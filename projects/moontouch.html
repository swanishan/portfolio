UX Research Accessibility Concept

MoonTouch

An accessibility tool with a revolutionary non-visual user interface that enhances the experience of smartphones for users with visual impairments.

Role

UX Researcher & Designer

Duration

6 months

Domain

Accessibility · Mobile

Status

Concept

The Problem

Visually impaired smartphone users rely heavily on screen readers, but the experience remains fragmented and inaccessible in many contexts. Existing tools struggle to convey spatial information, making tasks like navigating complex apps, browsing images, or interacting with maps frustrating or impossible.

The Approach

MoonTouch explores a haptic-first interaction model. Instead of relying on audio alone, the system uses vibration patterns, 3D touch, and tactile gestures to give users a richer sense of space, hierarchy, and feedback.

Key Insights

Through user interviews with 12 visually impaired participants, we found that haptic feedback was consistently under-utilized in mainstream phones, despite being one of the most intuitive channels for non-visual communication.

The Solution

A system of tactile patterns mapped to common UI elements — buttons, sliders, alerts, navigation. Users can feel their way through an interface the same way sighted users navigate with their eyes.

Outcome

Validated concept with strong user feedback — 9 out of 12 participants reported the prototype felt "more intuitive than VoiceOver" for spatial tasks. Concept received a UX Design Award nomination.
